Why do you want to work at Overstory?

Three things drew me to Overstory specifically.

First, the mission is real and urgent. I've spent the last decade building products in finance — custody platforms, digital banking, trading systems. The work was technically challenging, but the impact stayed inside a narrow world. Overstory is using technology to prevent wildfires and keep the grid running for communities. That's the kind of problem I want to put my energy into now.

Second, the stage and the challenge match what I do best. Post-Series B, scaling from 100 people, building out a technical strategy for the next 12–36 months — I've done this. At Ripple I grew the custody engineering team from 40 to 50 across Geneva and San Francisco, restructured into cross-functional squads, and partnered closely with Product to ship a complex, data-intensive platform. At Bazzile I built a product from zero — including geospatial data pipelines and real-time processing — so I understand what it takes at both ends of the scale. Overstory sits at the intersection of AI and remote sensing, which is a space where my hands-on data platform experience and my product-centric approach to engineering leadership can have real leverage.

Third, the way Overstory describes its culture — boldness, curiosity, empathy, listening — resonates with how I lead. I've consistently built diverse, high-performing teams (96% satisfaction at Ripple) by creating environments where people feel ownership and psychological safety. I care about hiring people who are smarter than me, giving them context instead of instructions, and getting out of their way. That seems to be exactly what Overstory values.

On a personal level, I have two children. The climate crisis isn't abstract for me — it's about what kind of world they'll grow up in. I want my work to contribute to a safer, more resilient future for them and for everyone's kids. Overstory gives me the chance to do that with the skills I've spent 15 years building.

In short: I want to work on something that matters, at a company that's at the right inflection point for my skills, with people who lead the way I do.
