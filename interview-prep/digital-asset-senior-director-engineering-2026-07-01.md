## Canton Network - Plain-English Explanation

Use this mental model: **Canton is trying to be the public blockchain for institutional finance, but without forcing banks to reveal all transaction data to everyone.**

Classic public chains such as Ethereum give composability and shared settlement, but they are too transparent for regulated capital markets. Private/permissioned ledgers solve confidentiality, but they usually recreate silos: one ledger for one consortium, another ledger for another workflow. Canton tries to keep both sides: a public interoperable network where institutions can connect applications and assets, while each party only sees the data it is entitled to see.

The core idea is a **network of networks**:

- **Daml** is the smart contract language and business-workflow model. It encodes who signs, who can act, who can observe, and what rules govern a contract.
- **Participant nodes** represent institutions or users. They hold each party's permitted view of the ledger, not the whole network's data.
- **Synchronizers / sync domains** order and synchronize transactions between participant nodes. Think of them as privacy-preserving, fault-tolerant coordination infrastructure, not as a database where everyone reads everything.
- **Global Synchronizer / Canton Foundation** provides neutral governance and shared interoperability for the public Canton Network.

The important technical difference: Canton does **not** replicate every transaction to every node. Each party sees a valid subset of a shared virtual ledger. If a transaction involves Bank A, Asset Issuer B, and Custodian C, those parties can validate the pieces they are entitled to see. Unrelated institutions do not see the details.

Why this matters for capital markets:

- **Privacy:** institutions can transact on shared infrastructure without revealing positions, counterparties, or client-sensitive details to the whole market.
- **Atomic settlement:** complex workflows can settle as all-or-nothing transactions, such as exchanging tokenized Treasuries against cash or stablecoins.
- **Interoperability:** applications can compose across assets and workflows instead of becoming isolated private ledgers.
- **Control and compliance:** issuers and regulated institutions can preserve permissions, governance, auditability, and jurisdictional constraints.
- **Scale:** applications can scale independently instead of competing for one global execution bottleneck.

Current market story to remember: Digital Asset raised $135M in June 2025, then announced a larger $355M round in June 2026 led by a16z crypto with participation from institutions including ABN Amro, Apollo, BNP Paribas, Broadridge, Citadel Securities, CME Ventures, Coinbase Ventures, HSBC, iCapital, Optiver, S&P Global, SBI Group, SoFi and Tradeweb. Canton is being positioned around tokenized real-world assets, collateral mobility, settlement, payments and regulated financial workflows, including DTCC-targeted U.S. Treasury tokenization.
