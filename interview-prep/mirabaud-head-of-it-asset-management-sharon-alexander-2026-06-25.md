## Likely HRBP Questions

### "How did the previous interview go from your side?"

> Very well. What I appreciated was the realism of the discussion. Raphaël looked at IT as a business-enabling function, and Pierre brought the operational and transition reality. That is important for me because I am not interested in an abstract transformation role; I want a mandate where continuity, risks, providers, users and business priorities are discussed openly.

### "Why Mirabaud?"

> Mirabaud is attractive because it is local, established, independent, and close to the kind of financial environments where I have done my best work. The role is also not one-dimensional: it combines run, infrastructure, data, applications, governance, budget, providers and transformation for Asset Management. That mix is rare, and it matches my FlowBank, Pictet AM and UBP experience.

### "Why this role after Director / Head of Technology roles?"

> The title is less important to me than the real perimeter. This role is interesting because it owns the IT function for Asset Management, with business proximity and real operational responsibility. My seniority is useful here because the transition needs someone who can build trust quickly, understand risk, speak to business stakeholders, and still go deep enough with technical teams and providers.

### "Your recent roles look short. Should we worry about stability?"

> It is a fair question. The recent shorter experiences were specific mandates around transformation, AI adoption and Field CTO work. They helped me clarify that I want a durable internal leadership role again, not only advisory or presales. The Mirabaud role is exactly the kind of longer-term mandate I am looking for: local, regulated, business-facing, and with ownership.

### "What is your management style?"

> Structured, transparent and calm. I like teams to know what matters, what is expected, where risks are, and how decisions are made. I am close enough to the work to challenge technical choices, but I do not micromanage strong people. At Ripple / Metaco I led up to 50 people across managers, tech leads and engineers, with clear squads, L3 ownership, quality practices and high team satisfaction.

### "How do you handle a transition with an outgoing incumbent?"

> With respect and method. I would first capture what is explicit and implicit: critical systems, vendors, recurring incidents, business relationships, historical decisions, fragile dependencies and current commitments. The goal is not to erase Pierre's work; it is to preserve the knowledge, protect continuity, and then create a clear roadmap with the COO and stakeholders.

### "What kind of culture do you need to succeed?"

> I work best in a culture that values clarity, trust and accountability. I do not need a huge organization or a flashy transformation label. I need access to the right stakeholders, honest prioritization, and enough autonomy to connect business needs, risk, budget and execution.

### "What are your compensation expectations?"

Use only if asked.

> I would first like to understand Mirabaud's band and package structure for this role. To be transparent, I mentioned earlier that CHF 160k base would be my bare minimum to preserve my current living standard, especially because the role is in Geneva rather than Zurich and my family situation has changed with the children now in public school. But that is a floor, not my expectation for a Head of IT mandate.
>
> For this kind of role, with IT ownership, budget, providers, governance and transformation, I would naturally expect something above that minimum and aligned with the real scope of responsibility. I am not coming in asking for CHF 250k base, but I would like the package to reflect the seniority and accountability of the role. What band has Mirabaud planned?

If she pushes for minimum:

> The absolute minimum base I could make work is CHF 160k, but I would not want that number to become the anchor for the role if the scope is truly Head of IT. I would rather align around Mirabaud's band, the full package, and the level of accountability expected.

### "Availability?"

> I am Geneva-based, I have a C permit, and I can align on a practical start date once we confirm mutual fit and process timing.
