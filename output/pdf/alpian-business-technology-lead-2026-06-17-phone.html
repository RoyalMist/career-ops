Likely Questions

How would you identify AI opportunities in Compliance?

  1. Start with workflow discovery, not model selection.
  2. Map volumes, manual decisions, rework, SLA pain, audit evidence and exceptions.
  3. Separate low-risk summarization, medium-risk triage, and high-risk recommendations.
  4. Design for prompt/version history, input/output capture, human decision log, policy references, access controls and rollback.
  5. Measure time to prepare case, time to decision, false positives, evidence quality, adoption and rework.
I would not start with "where can we put an LLM?" I would start with where analysts lose time, where evidence is hard to assemble, and where decisions need better traceability.

Likely Questions

Design an AI-assisted KYC or transaction monitoring workflow.

  • Inputs: profile, documents, transaction history, rules, policies, sanctions/PEP/adverse media, prior notes.
  • Processing: deterministic rules stay deterministic; AI summarizes, classifies, detects missing evidence, retrieves policy and drafts rationale.
  • Human loop: analyst approves, rejects or edits. No autonomous adverse decision.
  • Controls: RBAC, data minimization, model/prompt versioning, audit logs, traceable citations, reviewer sign-off.
  • Rollout: shadow mode, limited scope, quality sampling, rollback.
The AI prepares and structures the evidence; accountable humans make regulated decisions.

Likely Questions

How do you translate business requirements into implementable specs?

  • Start from the operational outcome and constraints.
  • Write acceptance criteria in business and technical terms.
  • Identify data sources, system boundaries, failure modes, ownership, security controls and release path.
  • Validate with engineering before committing dates.
  • Create metrics before building.
A good specification is a shared decision record: problem, non-goals, data, controls, success criteria and production ownership.

How do you work with engineers when you are not the line manager?

  • Bring clarity, not authority theatre.
  • Involve engineers early in problem framing.
  • Separate discovery from delivery commitment.
  • Let engineers challenge feasibility and risk.
  • Make trade-offs explicit for business stakeholders.
  • Protect engineering from vague urgency while keeping accountability.
My job is to reduce ambiguity before it hits engineering, and then keep enough context flowing so engineers can make good technical trade-offs.

Likely Questions

Where have you transformed a business with AI or automation?

Use SCRYPT: fragmented execution across Slack/email/meetings became Claude Enterprise integrated with Slack, Linear, email and calendars, plus company-wide Linear and clearer delivery/L3 ownership.

Bridge: apply the same pattern to Compliance/KYC/transaction monitoring by improving evidence preparation, triage and workflow visibility.

How do you make sure AI is safe in a regulated bank?

  • Classify use cases by decision risk.
  • Keep high-impact decisions human-owned.
  • Make data access explicit and minimal.
  • Keep complete audit trail.
  • Test on historical cases before production.
  • Monitor drift, hallucination, rework and user overrides.
  • Involve Compliance, Risk, Legal and Security early.
The model is only one component. The system also includes data quality, access, human approval, audit evidence, monitoring and ownership.
