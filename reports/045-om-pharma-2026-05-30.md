## C) Level and Strategy

**Level detected:** Director-level Head of IT, reporting directly to the CTO, leading 17 people for headquarters and biotech production-site IT.

**Candidate natural level:** Strong fit. The role maps to Global Head of Technology, CTO/CPO, Head of Engineering and regulated enterprise technology leadership. It is narrower than CTO/VP Engineering, but more compelling than the recent 5-person local Head of IT roles because it has CTO reporting, 17 people, GxP/security, CAPEX/OPEX and enterprise digital initiatives.

**Sell senior without lying**

- "I have already led a full regulated technology department of around 40 people, covering software engineering, data, infrastructure, service delivery, vendors, roadmap and budget."
- "My strongest angle for OM Pharma is practical IT leadership: governance, cybersecurity, infrastructure, systems, CAPEX/OPEX, providers, service quality and business-aligned digital initiatives."
- "I do not come from a pure pharma/GxP career. I do bring regulated banking, audit, GDPR/IAM, custody, production operations and executive technology governance."
- "I can help make IT a business partner while keeping operations stable, secure and compliant."

**If they downscope me**

Continue only if the role has real decision rights. Ask for:

1. Compensation band and bonus structure.
2. Reporting cadence with the CTO and Executive Committee.
3. IT team composition: infrastructure, applications, support, security, business systems.
4. Scope of GxP systems, validation and quality responsibilities.
5. Enterprise digital initiatives already planned for the next 12-18 months.
6. Expected hands-on load versus leadership/governance load.
