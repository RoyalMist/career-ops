## C) Level and Strategy

Detected level: senior ICT business partner / local ICT manager.

Candidate natural level: CTO, Field CTO, VP Engineering, Head/Director Engineering, or high-impact strategic Enterprise/Solution Architect.

This is not a north-star role. It is closer to a local business-facing ICT leadership mandate with some transformation content. The JD language is attractive - "transformation digitale", "sparring partner", "alignement stratégique ICT" - but the concrete team size and task list are modest. It mentions one support lead and two project managers, local ICT coordination, local projects, and small operational activities.

Sell senior without lying:

- Lead with regulated environments: FlowBank, UBP, Ripple / Metaco.
- Frame clinical digitalization as high-trust process transformation rather than "healthcare expertise".
- Position the candidate as someone who can align clinic leadership, central ICT, local delivery, governance, and service quality.
- Avoid over-indexing on AI. Mention AI adoption only as a modernization lever if Hirslanden is ready for it.

If they downlevel:

- Accept only if the role has real Romandie-wide authority, clear budget/project governance, and compensation at or above the practical minimum.
- Negotiate 80%-90% if compensation is below private-sector target.
- Ask for a 6-month review tied to strategic portfolio ownership, not only operational service quality.
