## C) Level and Strategy

**Detected level:** Senior public-sector SI domain responsible / business information systems urbanization lead.

**Candidate's natural level:** CTO / VP Engineering / Head of Engineering / Director / Field CTO / strategic Enterprise Architect.

This is not a bad role, but it is a smaller mandate than the target lane. It buys architecture, stakeholder coordination, risk analysis and roadmap supervision. It does not appear to buy full technology-department ownership, people-management scale, budget ownership, build-vs-buy authority or executive operating-model leadership.

**Sell senior without lying**

- Lead with FlowBank target architecture and ExCo roadmap ownership, but frame it as "schema directeur SI, diagnostic d'existant, trajectoire de migration".
- Use UBP for public-service style rigor: audit evidence, risk, governance, remediation and French/English reporting.
- Use CREA to make the education domain credible without pretending to have managed Swiss school administration systems.
- Keep AI secondary. The DGNSI has a digital transformation agenda, but this JD is not an AI role.

**If they downlevel or the package is weak**

Accept only if the compensation is at least near the public-sector threshold, the role has real transverse authority across services, and there is a path toward broader DGNSI architecture / domain leadership. Otherwise, it risks becoming an underpaid governance-and-documentation role.
