Candidate natural level: CTO, Head of Engineering, Director Software Engineering, Field CTO, AI transformation leader. The level maps well. The function is more institutional policy and global programme leadership than software product/engineering leadership.

Positioning:

- Lead with "AI and technology executive who has operationalized AI in regulated Swiss environments."
- Make the bridge from regulated banking/crypto/finance governance to responsible AI in health.
- Emphasize AI adoption, governance, architecture, human-in-the-loop controls, risk posture, and executive stakeholder alignment.
- Use the Geneva base and French/English language profile as a strong practical advantage.
- Do not over-index on hands-on coding; WHO is buying credibility, policy translation, governance and programme leadership.

Main strategic question: whether WHO will consider a strong AI/technology executive without explicit global health and publication credentials. The role wording is unusually demanding on AI-for-health reputation, which may favor candidates already visible in WHO, academia, public health, digital health NGOs, or global health AI initiatives.
