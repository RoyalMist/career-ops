## C) Level and Strategy

**Level detected:** Mid-Senior Digital Platforms Manager / Product Owner. The role owns roadmap, user stories, app development coordination, KPIs and releases, but does not clearly own engineering, architecture, budget, team, enterprise systems or executive technology strategy.

**Candidate natural level:** CTO / Head of Engineering / Director Software Engineering. The candidate can do the role, but it would likely underuse the profile.

**Sell senior without lying**

- "I have led mobile and platform products from strategy through production, including architecture, APIs, cloud and delivery."
- "My strongest fit would be if this role owns a broader digital platform portfolio and not only coordination of a travel app roadmap."
- "I do not come from luxury hospitality, but I have built client-facing digital products and led platforms where reliability, user experience and business alignment mattered."

**If they downlevel**

Do not accept a pure product-owner/platform-manager scope unless compensation, title, budget and decision rights are closer to Head of Digital Platforms. Ask for reporting line, team size, vendor ownership and whether this is a strategic platform role or a delivery coordinator role.
